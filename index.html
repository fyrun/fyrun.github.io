Free Your Run

It's your fitness data.
Time to take it to the next level.

The Mission

Garmin Connect users create vast amounts of personal data each day. Workouts, sleep, steps, all-day heart rate, locations, and more. What if you could do more with your data?

Your data stored with Garmin has the potential to give you innovative insights into your health, fitness, and activities. Unlocking ways that you can improve your performance, measure personal growth, and celebrate the achievement of your goals.

But you can't access your data.

Today, using Garmin Connect's API is locked behind a $5,000 paywall. This prevents you and other creative teams from unleashing the insights behind your fitness data.

What's an API?

It is a way for your fitness data, with your permission, to be accessed by other applications or used in your own personal analysis. The map behind was generated by the folks at Strava Labs using aggregated workout tracking data.

Just think what you could do with your own data. But you can't, because of Garmin's $5000 paywall.

You could also use any of these applications, created by small teams, which have been limited by Garmin's ransom for your data.

  • SmashRun
  • Golden Cheetah
  • ConnectStats
  • TrailForks
  • Fluxtream
Want to be included in this list? Tweet us.

Why should this be free?

Other fitness services Strava, Runkeeper, Fitbit and more make your fitness data usable in the way you best see fit. For free.

Isn't it time Garmin gave you the freedom to use your own data without a $5,000 admission fee?

But don't just take our word for it.

The Guardian
How activity trackers remove our rights to our most intimate data

"It's bad enough that APIs usually offer access to a limited amount of data, but Garmin now ransoms it out at $5,000 a go. This means small software developers and coders who might want to design an app, or even someone who wants to get a complete dataset into a spreadsheet, have to pay through the nose for the right to do so. That’s hardly accessible to the not-for-profits and passionate bedroom coders who do some of the most interesting and innovative work in this brave new data rich world."
